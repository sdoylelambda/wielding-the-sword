Wielding the Sword

My Story of Faith and Healing

By Sue Stanley

Buy Now

Some might call me a woman of faith because I wholeheartedly believe in the power of God’s promises, but I know of many good faithful people who believed, yet died in their sickness. This is my story, one that doubters can’t change, it happened to me, and I’m alive today because of it. Please come with me as I share my journey, in the hope that it will help, encourage, and strengthen you. Before I begin though, I want you to know that I was under a doctor’s care, went through all the treatments, bore the side effects of chemo, and know that was a key part of my recovery—I’m not in denial of the goodness of medical treatment, but it must be coupled with the belief that God is faithful to His word.

About Sue Stanley

Sue and her husband Dale have lived in the FL Panhandle since 1992. They have been blessed with two children, 7 grandchildren and 1 great-grandson. They celebrated their 50th Wedding Anniversary Aug 2024.

Confessions of Faith

Bible verses that gave me strength

Mark 11:22, Have Faith in God
Confession: Thank you Jesus, I lift you up and I acknowledge you in every aspect of my life. I thank you for the word that you put in my mouth. You said through King Solomon, death and life are in the power of the tongue (Prov 18:21). I lift You up and praise You for all the mighty things You are doing in my body. I speak life to my body and God You know I trust in You and have faith in You and I glorify Your name and thank You for never leaving me. Amen.

Jer 29:11, For I know the plans I have for you saith the Lord. Plans to prosper you and not to harm you. Plans to give you a future and a hope.
Confession: Father God, I praise You and I know that You are a good God and only good. I know that this sickness did not come from Your hand for Your plans are to prosper me and never to harm me. You have a plan, a path laid out for me and I have to stay on the path that is lit by the word (Ps 119:105). Father, I am walking with You and know that Your plan for me is a future for you promised it to me. Amen.

Is 55:11, So shall My word be that goes forth from my mouth. It shall not return to me void, but it shall accomplish what I please, And it shall prosper in the thing for which I sent it.
Confession: Father God, I am thankful that I can speak Your word with the same mindset that it will accomplish and achieve what you sent it to accomplish. Your word will achieve the purpose. Through Your word I receive Your promises and the benefits of forgiveness, healing, and redemption. Ps 103 says You crown me, You satisfy my mouth with good things, and You renew my youth like that of an eagle. Father God, I am going to prosper because You are not a man that You should lie nor the son of man that You should repent (Num 23:19). I trust in You my God and Savior. Amen.

Mal 3:6, For I am the Lord, I do not change.
Confession: Father God, I come before You to lift up Your name and exalt You. I am standing on Your word that does not change. I come to You and delight myself in Your word. Jesus, You are the faithful witness and Isaiah prophesied that You are called Wonderful, Counselor, Mighty God, the Everlasting Father, the Prince of Peace, of the increase of his Government and peace there would be no end (Is 9:6). Praise God that You do not change. Amen.

Heb 13:8, Jesus Christ is the same yesterday, today and forever.
Confession: Dear Heavenly Father, I am here before You praising You for Your faithfulness. So very thankful for Your word that I can stand on knowing it is never changing. You will never leave me, You go before me and make the crooked places straight (Is 45:2), You comfort me, You prepare a table for me in the very presence of my enemies (Ps 23:5). Thank you for your faithfulness in my life. Amen.

Ps 119:89, Forever, O Lord, Your word is settled in heaven.
Confession: Dear Heavenly Father I come before You and return Your word to You. Your word is established and will not change. I recognize the power of the word is to: rule my life, recognize the authority in my life, to recognize the power in my tongue, and to recognize my position of authority in the royal family. For You have said that I am a chosen generation, a royal priesthood, a holy nation, His own special people, that I will proclaim the praises of Him who brought me out of darkness, into His marvelous light (I Pet 2:9). Amen.

Deut 31:8, And the Lord He is the one who goes before you. He will be with you. He will not leave you nor forsake you, do not fear nor be dismayed.
Confession: Father God, I rejoice at Your word and I am bringing Your word to You. I know that You are always with me and You cover me with Your feathers (Ps 91:4) and You uphold me with Your mighty right hand (Is 41:10). I praise you for Your favor, for You, O Lord, will bless the righteous, with favor You will surround them as with a shield (Ps 5:12). Father God, You are with me through the valley of the shadow of death but I am moving through the valley, I am not staying here. I am coming out victorious for You are my healer, my strength, and my salvation. Amen.

I Sam 17:45-46, Then David said to the Philistine, You come to me with a sword, with a spear, and with a javelin. But I come to you in the name of the Lord of hosts, the God of the armies of Israel, whom you have defied. 46) This day the Lord will deliver you into my hand, and I will strike you and take your head from you.
Confession: Father God I praise Your almighty name. Thank You for showing me to take out my sword and cut off the enemy’s head with the word of God! Thank You for giving me Your word and allowing me to speak it clearly to my fear, to the Dr. report, to the sickness in my body. I stand healed and praise Your Holy Name. Amen.

Ps 107:20, He sent His word and healed them, And delivered them from their destructions.
Confession: Father God, I come into Your gates with thanksgiving and into Your courts with praise. I am thankful for Your word that was sent to heal. Your word I have hidden in my heart that I might not sin against You (Ps 119:11). Your word is hidden in my heart and out of the abundance of the heart the mouth speaks (Luke 6:45). Father show me how to guard the word in my heart, and to speak it and to stand on it and to see it come to pass. Show me how to pull on Your word that I speak in the spiritual and pull it into the natural world that I will see it, for now faith is the substance of things hoped for the evidence of things not seen (Heb 11:1). I will see my healing come to pass, for I have spoken Your word that was sent to heal. Amen.

Job 22:28, You will also declare a thing. And it will be established for you, So light will shine on your ways.
Confession:Heavenly Father, thank You for Your love for Your people. Thank You that You see me and You know me. I come before You and I know that I have Your benefits and You are pouring favor upon me. Help me not to get stuck in my natural flesh but to set my mind on those things above not on the things on the earth (Col 3:2). I cannot react to what is being said concerning my body. I am setting my mind on what Your word says about me. Your word is truth that is being revealed to me. Speak deeply into the core of my being and illuminate Your word. I believe that Your word is being manifested in my body and I am the healed of the Lord. Amen

Bio

Sue loves to sing and is a member of the Worship Team at Champion Life Church in FWB, FL and is also a member of the Air Force Armament Quartet who is asked to sing the National Anthem at Promotion Ceremonies, Retirement Ceremonies, POW/MIA Ceremonies, and at the 5-Flags Speedway in Pensacola from time to time.

Sue attended the In Christ International Bible Institute and has fallen in love with the word of God, making it her anchor. As you can see in the book ‘Wielding the Sword’, she stood on the word of God and gives thanks to God for the miracle she saw in her own life.

Sue loves to do crafts and spends a lot of time in her craft room busy making wreaths, scrapbooking, making cards, flower arrangements, decoupaging with napkins, and loves trying out new ideas. Sue loves searching Pinterest!

Sue is a member of the Daughters of the American Revolution (DAR) and enjoyed researching the genealogy of her patriot member that served in the Revolutionary War.
